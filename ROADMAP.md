# Public Roadmap Summary

Status: Sanitized public-review draft
Date: June 13, 2026

## Principle

Keep the first build narrow enough to prove and broad enough to scale.

## Phase 1: Legitimacy Review

Goal: strengthen the standards before industry, infrastructure, foundation, or agency outreach.

Outputs:

- reviewer feedback;
- anti-capture revisions;
- stronger no-go terms;
- clearer evidence requirements;
- clearer student, worker, data, community, and ecological protections.

## Phase 2: Public-Interest Alignment

Goal: map the standards to public-interest institutions and practical review pathways.

Potential reviewers:

- student and faculty groups;
- public-interest AI researchers;
- data-rights and civil-liberties groups;
- labor and worker-transition voices;
- participatory budgeting practitioners;
- environmental and watershed reviewers;
- community governance practitioners.

## Phase 3: Agency and Foundation Mapping

Goal: understand how the standards map to real programs, grants, procurement language, evaluation methods, and public-sector needs.

Output:

- public-interest concept note;
- evidence-room budget;
- pilot criteria;
- procurement and policy language draft.

## Phase 4: Bounded Technical Proponents

Goal: invite AI, infrastructure, and technical actors only into bounded roles after legitimacy review.

Rules:

- no ownership transfer;
- no exclusive rights;
- no use of marks without permission;
- no model-training rights over protected data;
- no self-certification;
- no public-benefit claims without evidence.

## Phase 5: Minimum Viable Public Receipt

Goal: prove one thing clearly.

The first proof should show:

1. AI or AI-enabling infrastructure created measurable value or avoided measurable harm.
2. Affected people governed the decision.
3. A defined share of value was routed into visible benefit.
4. Risks were logged.
5. The result was independently reviewable.

## Phase 6: Replicable Pilot

Goal: turn the first proof into a reusable pilot method.

Possible contexts:

- campus AI governance;
- public-benefit workflow;
- participatory budgeting support;
- responsible AI infrastructure review;
- data-trust dashboard;
- community resilience planning.

## Scalable Horizon

The longer-term vision includes Common Ground standards, evidence tooling, data trusts, local cooperatives, responsible AI infrastructure guardrails, ecological accountability, student governance, and multi-site federation.

The standard should scale only where evidence, affected-party governance, and enforceable benefit routing exist.
