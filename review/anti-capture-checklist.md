# Anti-Capture Review Checklist

Status: Public-review draft
Date: June 13, 2026

## Purpose

This checklist helps reviewers identify whether the Common Ground Standard drafts could be captured by powerful actors, diluted into public-relations language, or used without real community, student, worker, data, or ecological accountability.

## Governance Capture

- [ ] Does any actor gain control over the standard?
- [ ] Does any actor gain unilateral control over public claims?
- [ ] Are affected people represented with decision power, not just consultation?
- [ ] Are student, worker, community, and ecological voices included early enough?
- [ ] Are pause, appeal, or no-go rights clear?
- [ ] Is there a path for independent review?

## Data Capture

- [ ] Are data rights explicit?
- [ ] Is model training prohibited unless explicitly approved?
- [ ] Are student, worker, community, tribal, and ecological data treated as protected?
- [ ] Is consent revocable?
- [ ] Are data retention and access controls defined?
- [ ] Is raw sensitive data excluded from public release?

## Evidence Capture

- [ ] Are claims tied to baseline evidence?
- [ ] Are public dashboards separated from independent audit?
- [ ] Are failures and limitations publishable?
- [ ] Can a funder or platform suppress general evidence?
- [ ] Are public benefit claims prohibited until benefit routing is documented?
- [ ] Is endorsement clearly separated from review?

## Economic Capture

- [ ] Does the benefit clause protect workers from replacement-with-compensation framing?
- [ ] Are affected people involved in benefit-routing decisions?
- [ ] Are benefits more than public relations spending?
- [ ] Are savings measured against a credible baseline?
- [ ] Are long-term obligations clear?
- [ ] Are low-quality service cuts excluded from claimed surplus?

## Infrastructure Capture

- [ ] Are grid, water, and retail-bill impacts independently reviewable?
- [ ] Are no-go conditions enforceable?
- [ ] Are community benefit agreements binding?
- [ ] Are data-center or infrastructure actors prevented from self-certifying?
- [ ] Are ecological safeguards tied to remedies?
- [ ] Are workforce and student transition benefits included?

## Campus Capture

- [ ] Do students have real governance authority?
- [ ] Are authorship rules clear and non-punitive?
- [ ] Is hidden surveillance prohibited?
- [ ] Are campus workers included?
- [ ] Are vendor data and model-training terms disclosed?
- [ ] Are appeal rights defined?

## Ecological Capture

- [ ] Are ecological commitments enforceable?
- [ ] Are restoration triggers defined?
- [ ] Are Rights-of-Nature claims used carefully and legally?
- [ ] Is mitigation separated from permission to harm?
- [ ] Are ecological guardians independent?
- [ ] Are sensor and telemetry claims audited?

## Reviewer Conclusion

Based on this checklist:

- What should be strengthened before public outreach?
- What should be removed?
- What should be a hard no-go condition?
- What would prevent this from becoming reputation cover?
