Subject 03 / Public-review draft

AI-created gains should be proven, governed, and shared.

Savings or productivity claims should not be accepted at face value. A credible benefit path starts with a documented baseline, protects workers and services, gives affected people decision authority, and leaves a public record of what happened.

Test a benefit claimRead the formal draft

Public review only. This draft does not prove that gains exist, authorize replacement harm, or imply endorsement of an implementing organization.

In plain language

The benefit clause in one minute

When AI creates measurable value, affected people should help decide how that value is used. The gain should be checked before it is claimed, and sharing a benefit should never erase labor, data, privacy, appeal, or service obligations.

01 / Measure

Prove the gain

Use a documented baseline, method, costs, assumptions, quality effects, and review period.

02 / Protect

Do not trade away rights

Preserve labor, data, privacy, appeal, human accountability, and service quality.

03 / Decide

Share authority

Workers, students, service recipients, or communities help choose the benefit path.

04 / Publish

Leave a public receipt

Show what was measured, decided, routed, delivered, limited, and reviewed.

01

Measurement baseline

Is the claimed gain real, additional, and measured against a credible starting point?

What could go wrong?
An implementing organization can overstate savings, ignore new costs, or count ordinary improvements as AI-created value.
What proof is needed?
A documented baseline, method, time period, assumptions, total costs, quality effects, and independent checking.
Who needs protection?
Affected people need access to the method and a way to challenge what was counted or left out.
When should it pause?
When the gain cannot be separated from cost cutting, service decline, transferred work, or unsupported assumptions.

Formal public draft

Model clause under review

Status: public-review one-pager. This is a proposed accountability clause, not evidence that a particular deployment created value or delivered a benefit.

Core principle

When AI creates measurable productivity, cost, quality, access, or resilience gains, affected people should help govern how those gains are used.

01

Measure the gain

Use a documented baseline and method.

02

Disclose assumptions

Publish material assumptions, limitations, and risks.

03

Share governance

Give affected workers, students, service recipients, or community representatives authority over routing decisions.

04

Allocate a defined share

Route verified value into an approved benefit pathway.

05

Publish a receipt

Show measured value, governance decision, routed benefit, and review status without exposing sensitive data.

06

Preserve protections

Keep data rights, privacy, labor protections, appeal rights, and human accountability intact.

Possible benefit pathways

  • Direct dividends
  • Emergency aid
  • Bill relief
  • Student support
  • Worker transition funds
  • Training funds
  • Service improvements
  • Cooperative ownership
  • Community resilience
  • Ecological restoration
  • Debt relief
  • Public-benefit reinvestment

Proven gains

Value measured against a documented baseline and checked before being shared or claimed.

Governance authority

Affected people have defined decision power, not only a chance to comment.

Public receipt

A non-sensitive record of what was measured, decided, delivered, limited, and reviewed.

What alignment should not mean

It should not make replacement acceptable because a small benefit is paid, weaken bargaining or data rights, excuse lower-quality services, accept savings without evidence, or let the implementing party control benefit routing alone.
