One-Sentence Thesis

Pivot is building a public-interest standard and evidence framework for making AI deployment and AI-enabling infrastructure prove measurable human, community, worker, student, and ecological benefit.

Why this review comes first

AI adoption is accelerating faster than public legitimacy. Students, workers, communities, agencies, and foundations need a way to distinguish credible public benefit from vague AI-for-good language before major industry outreach begins.

Public doctrine
  • No AI deployment without human agency.
  • No AI infrastructure without community benefit.
  • No AI productivity gain without a dividend path.
  • No data extraction without data-trust governance.
  • No ecological burden without transparency, restoration, and enforceable guardrails.
  • No AI education system without student authorship and learning integrity.
First review drafts

Responsible AI Infrastructure, Campus Common Ground Compact, and the AI Dividend / Benefit Clause are the first public-review materials.

Operating loop

Measure value, govern with affected people, route visible benefit, audit the result, and scale only what proves legitimate.

Current status

Standards-draft ready and public-review ready. Not certified, endorsed, operationally proven at scale, or claiming active pilots unless separately documented.

Desired review outcome

Clearer no-go terms, stronger safeguards, sharper evidence requirements, better anti-capture language, practical first-pilot criteria, and stronger public accountability.

What To Test

The strongest feedback identifies where the draft is too vague, too easy to capture, too difficult to verify, or too weak to protect affected people and places.
