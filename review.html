One-Sentence Thesis

The Common Ground Standard is a public-review draft and evidence framework for making AI deployment and AI-enabling infrastructure prove measurable human, community, worker, student, and ecological benefit.

Why this review comes first

AI adoption is accelerating faster than public legitimacy. Students, workers, communities, agencies, and foundations require standards to distinguish credible public benefit from vague AI-for-good language before major industry outreach is established.

Public doctrine
  • No AI deployment without human agency.
  • No AI infrastructure without community benefit.
  • No AI productivity gain without a dividend path.
  • No data extraction without data-trust governance.
  • No ecological burden without transparency, restoration, and enforceable guardrails.
  • No AI education system without preserving student authorship and learning integrity.
Origin and stewardship

Common Ground Standard is incubated by AI-Bio Synergy Holdings LLC during public review while independent governance and fiscal pathways are evaluated. This review is conducted through the public site and GitHub repository.

Deeper review boundary

The default review surface is public. Institutional or longer-form review can be requested, but confidential, restricted, partner, legal, technical, or data-room materials are not shared by default.

First review drafts

Responsible AI Infrastructure, Campus Common Ground Compact, and the AI Dividend / Benefit Clause are the first public-review materials.

Operating loop

Measure value, governance aligned with affected people, route visible benefit, audit the result, and scale only what proves legitimate.

Current status

Standards-draft ready and public-review ready. Not certified, endorsed, operationally proven at scale, or claiming active pilots unless separately documented.

Desired review outcome

Clearer no-go terms, stronger safeguards, sharper evidence requirements, better anti-capture language, practical first-pilot criteria, and stronger public accountability.

What To Test

The strongest feedback identifies where the draft is too vague, too easy to capture, too difficult to verify, or too weak to protect affected people and places.
