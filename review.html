Public review brief

Help test the draft before anyone asks you to trust it.

Common Ground Standard is asking a practical question: what should people be able to see, question, protect, and change before an AI system or infrastructure project is described as responsible?

Find my review pathChoose a subject
What we are asking forCritique, not agreement.

Point to a gap, a weak safeguard, an unsupported claim, a missing voice, or a rule that would not work in practice.

What we are not asking for

Endorsement, certification, sign-on, partnership, funding, or confidential information.

Why review comes first

AI is moving faster than shared rules for public trust.

Students, workers, communities, public agencies, foundations, and institutions need ways to distinguish real public benefit from broad AI-for-good language. This draft is being opened early so its weak points can be found before broader foundation, agency, industry, infrastructure, or implementation outreach.

The work is not finished by design. A useful review makes the next version harder to misuse, easier to understand, and more accountable to the people and places affected.

What the draft is trying to protect

Six promises worth pressure-testing.

  • 01

    AI deployment should preserve human agency.

  • 02

    AI infrastructure should return visible community benefit.

  • 03

    AI-created gains should have a fair benefit path.

  • 04

    Data use should remain under clear, enforceable governance.

  • 05

    Ecological burdens should be visible, bounded, and repairable.

  • 06

    AI education should preserve student authorship and learning integrity.

What useful feedback looks like

Specific beats comprehensive.

01

Name the concern

Point to the claim, safeguard, term, or process that needs work.

02

Name who is affected

Explain whose rights, work, learning, services, resources, or trust are at stake.

03

Name the missing test

Suggest the evidence, authority, appeal, public record, or no-go condition required.

04

Offer a practical change

Recommend clearer language or a safeguard that can be checked in real use.

Where your feedback goes

Use the website unless you prefer the public source record.

The website form is the primary path and does not require a GitHub account. GitHub remains available for reviewers who want to discuss a public issue or propose a source edit. Longer institutional review can be requested through Contact.
