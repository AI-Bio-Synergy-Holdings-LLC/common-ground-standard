Stewardship during public review

Who is holding the work while public review is open?

AI-Bio Synergy Holdings LLC is the current founder steward and project incubator. It keeps the draft, domain, repository, marks, and working materials coordinated while independent governance and fiscal options are evaluated.

What is true todayFounder-stewarded and publicly reviewable.

The public draft and change history are visible. Stewardship responsibilities are named rather than presented as neutral or independent.

What is not claimed

Common Ground Standard is not yet an independent standards body, nonprofit, certification authority, or representative council for affected communities.

The current role

Keep the work coherent without pretending the final structure already exists.

Early public work needs someone to hold the rights, maintain the source record, protect the name, publish drafts, and keep public claims within the review boundary. AI-Bio Synergy Holdings LLC currently performs that role.

That stewardship is temporary by design. It does not give the company permanent control over a future standards body, independent review, certification, affected-party representation, or public-interest governance.

What stewardship covers today

A defined operating role, with visible limits.

01

Protect and maintain

Hold founder-controlled intellectual property, background materials, domains, repositories, and draft records.

02

Publish and reconcile

Keep the public website, public source record, and internal canonical documentation aligned.

03

Coordinate review

Receive public critique, maintain disclosure boundaries, and prepare the project for serious external scrutiny.

04

Plan separation

Evaluate governance, fiscal-host, conflict, and assurance structures that do not depend on founder control forever.

What must remain separate

Money, review, and public claims cannot buy each other.

  • Funding is not governance.A donor or sponsor should not gain control over the standard, reviewer selection, findings, or correction decisions.
  • Participation is not endorsement.Reviewing, advising, funding, or commenting does not authorize public claims of approval or partnership.
  • Stewardship is not certification.The current steward cannot use its operating role as proof that an organization or project meets the draft.
  • Access is not influence.Deeper review, if offered, should not create a private route for weakening public safeguards.

Why sponsor buttons are off for now

Public funding should wait until the rules are ready.

Common Ground Standard is not currently accepting public sponsorship through GitHub Sponsors or Open Collective. Before a funding link goes live, the project should identify a fiscal pathway, publish allowed uses of funds, state donor limits, define conflicts and approvals, and explain how financial support is kept separate from standards control.

The direction of travel

Build independence in stages, with evidence at each step.

The immediate task is to strengthen the public draft and reviewer experience. Later decisions about fiscal hosting, consortium governance, independent assurance, certification marks, or nonprofit structures should follow documented need, legal review, conflict controls, and explicit public boundaries.
