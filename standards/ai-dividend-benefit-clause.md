# AI Dividend / Benefit Clause

Status: Public-review one-pager
Date: June 13, 2026

## Purpose

The AI Dividend / Benefit Clause ensures that verified AI productivity gains leave a public receipt and route a defined share into visible benefit.

It can be adapted for public agencies, universities, nonprofits, care networks, unions, community organizations, and responsible enterprise pilots.

## Core Principle

When AI creates measurable productivity, cost, quality, access, or resilience gains, affected people should help govern how those gains are used.

## Model Clause Summary

Where an AI system, automation workflow, or AI-enabled infrastructure deployment creates measurable savings, productivity gains, avoided costs, service-access improvements, or operational surplus, the implementing party should:

1. measure the gain using a documented baseline and method;
2. disclose material assumptions and risks;
3. provide affected workers, students, service recipients, or community representatives with governance authority over benefit-routing decisions;
4. allocate a defined share of verified value into an approved benefit pathway;
5. publish a non-sensitive public receipt showing measured value, governance decision, routed benefit, and review status;
6. preserve data rights, privacy, labor protections, appeal rights, and human accountability.

## Approved Benefit Pathways

- direct dividends;
- emergency aid;
- bill relief;
- student support;
- worker transition funds;
- training funds;
- service improvements;
- cooperative ownership;
- community resilience funds;
- ecological restoration;
- debt defense or debt relief;
- public-benefit reinvestment.

## What Reviewers Should Test

- How should "verified surplus" be defined?
- What share should be routed into benefit?
- Who must have governance authority?
- How do we avoid using benefits as cover for job cuts?
- What data rights are needed?
- What should be published in the public receipt?

## What Alignment Should Not Mean

Alignment should not mean:

- AI replacement is acceptable if a small benefit is paid;
- workers lose bargaining rights;
- public services can be degraded;
- data rights are waived;
- claimed savings are accepted without baseline evidence;
- benefit-routing is controlled only by the implementing party.
