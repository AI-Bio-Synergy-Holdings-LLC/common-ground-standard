# Campus Common Ground Compact

Status: Public-review one-pager
Date: June 13, 2026

## Purpose

The Campus Common Ground Compact gives students, faculty, campus workers, and universities a practical governance framework for human-centered AI adoption.

It treats students as co-governors of the AI transition, not passive users or obstacles.

## Core Principle

AI may support learning, research, accessibility, civic planning, and public benefit, but it must not replace human authorship, human judgment, student agency, worker dignity, or democratic governance.

## Compact Commitments

| Commitment | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Human authorship | Students must know when AI is allowed, prohibited, disclosed, or evaluated |
| Learning integrity | AI tools must strengthen explanation, reasoning, debate, and original work |
| Student data trust | Student prompts, submissions, learning data, advising data, and telemetry cannot be reused for model training without explicit governance |
| No-surprise automation | Students and workers must know when AI affects grading, advising, admissions, hiring, support, or surveillance |
| Anti-surveillance | AI adoption must not expand hidden monitoring, profiling, proctoring, or disciplinary automation |
| Worker dignity | AI should reduce burden and improve services, not hide staff cuts or degrade teaching and support |
| Benefit clause | Verified savings should support students, workers, tuition relief, emergency aid, training, or community benefit |
| Student governance seat | Students must have review or voting authority in campus AI procurement and deployment |

## First Pilot Options

- AI procurement review board
- student data trust
- no-surprise automation policy
- AI disclosure and authorship rubric
- student benefit-routing pilot
- campus AI public dashboard

## What Reviewers Should Test

- Does this protect students without freezing useful AI experimentation?
- Are student data rights strong enough?
- Does this protect campus workers?
- What appeal rights are needed?
- What should be prohibited outright?
- How should universities disclose vendor, energy, data, and model-training implications?

## What Alignment Should Not Mean

Alignment should not mean:

- vendors can train on student data;
- students accept unclear AI rules;
- AI surveillance is legitimized;
- workers are excluded from review;
- the campus is certified;
- reviewers endorse the university or vendor.
