# Review Process

Status: Public review workflow
Date: June 13, 2026
Public surface refreshed: July 3, 2026

## Purpose

The review process turns public-interest critique into a stronger v0.2 standard.

The first review phase prioritizes legitimacy actors before foundations, agencies, AI companies, infrastructure operators, or data-center actors.

## Who We Are Seeking Review From

Priority reviewers include:

- students and student governance bodies;
- faculty allies and campus workers;
- public-interest AI researchers;
- civil-liberties and data-rights advocates;
- labor and worker-transition voices;
- participatory budgeting practitioners;
- environmental and watershed reviewers;
- Rights-of-Nature and ecological governance reviewers;
- community governance practitioners.

## Review Modes

| Mode | Best for |
| --- | --- |
| Public website | orientation, review brief, draft map, stewardship boundary, and contact route |
| GitHub issue | specific clause critique, missing safeguard, evidence requirement, capture risk |
| Pull request | precise wording edit or structural change |
| Contact form or external memo | longer institutional review or coalition feedback, without confidential or restricted material |

## Triage Labels

The review team should classify incoming feedback by:

- `standard`
- `campus-compact`
- `ai-benefit-clause`
- `evidence`
- `anti-capture`
- `data-rights`
- `worker-protection`
- `student-rights`
- `ecology`
- `infrastructure`
- `no-go-condition`
- `v0.2-candidate`

## Review Criteria

Feedback is highest priority when it:

1. identifies a capture risk;
2. strengthens affected-party governance;
3. clarifies a no-go condition;
4. improves data rights or privacy protections;
5. strengthens worker or student protections;
6. adds a practical evidence requirement;
7. prevents public-benefit overclaiming;
8. makes the standard more usable without weakening accountability.

## v0.2 Decision Rules

A proposed change should be considered for v0.2 if it:

- is specific enough to implement;
- improves public accountability;
- does not weaken data, student, worker, community, or ecological safeguards;
- can be evaluated with evidence;
- does not create a backdoor for endorsement, certification, or partner capture.

## Review Cycle

Suggested initial cycle:

1. Open issue-based public review.
2. Invite 10-20 legitimacy reviewers.
3. Triage feedback weekly.
4. Group feedback by standard area.
5. Draft v0.2 change log.
6. Publish v0.2 with accepted changes, deferred questions, and unresolved risks.

## Output of Phase 1

The review phase should produce:

- issue log;
- anti-capture risk register;
- revised one-pagers;
- v0.2 roadmap;
- reviewer-informed no-go terms;
- minimum evidence requirements before any alignment claim.

## Deeper Review Boundary

The default review surface is public: common-ground-standard.org plus this repository.

Longer-form or institutional review requests may be considered case by case, but public review does not grant access to private repositories, data rooms, technical annexes, legal materials, partner materials, certification materials, or restricted records.
